Current Research Projects

Differential Privacy for Optimal Power Flow Datasets

When studying optimization problems in electrical networks, researchers often find it difficult to obtain network datasets accurately representing real, existing systems due to security and privacy concerns. For example, the electrical load demands of industrial customers within a network can shed light on their production and economic strategy.

To preserve the privacy of consumers' electricity demands, we inject Laplacian noise into loads for a given snapshot of the network. This injection can result in an infeasible network for optimal power flow; hence, we are developing optimization models to maintain privacy guarantees while preserving the feasibility of the resulting obfuscated dataset.

Phase Transitions for Optimality Gaps in Optimal Power Flow

AC optimal power flow (ACOPF) is a defining problem of modern electrical systems. It involves finding the most economical generator dispatch satisfying electricity demands while meeting safety and operational constraints. ACOPF is a challenging problem proven to be NP-hard, and over the years relaxations such as the Semi-Definite Programming relaxation and the Quadratic Convex relaxation have been developed to provide relatively accurate solutions that converge faster.

In the past, research has shown the existence of optimality gaps (the distance between a primal solution and the relaxation bound) in test cases, and we extend these results by investigating and demonstrating the existence of significant optimality gaps with interesting phase transitions under different network conditions.
