CS@UMich

I am a rising senior at the University of Michigan, majoring in computer science with a minor in mathematics. Currently, I am a Kiluk Proof Tutor for MATH 217 (Linear Algebra), the gateway course for mathematics majors at Michigan.

In the Fall 2019 semester, I interned with Trillium Secure, a connected and autonomous vehicle cybersecurity startup, through the University of Michigan Transportation Research Institute.

In the past, I was a Seth Bonder Undergraduate at the University of Michigan working under the direction of Professor Pascal Van Hentenryck. My research focused on optimization problems related to power systems and differential privacy.

In Winter 2018, I was an Instructional Aide for ENGR 100-950, Electronics for Atmospheric and Space Measurements taught by Professor Aaron Ridley. Students in this class design and build a weather balloon payload housing a variety of atmospheric sensors. Payloads are deployed on a balloon to 100,000 ft, then recovered to analyze data collected by the integrated sensors.

In Fall 2017, I was a member of TechLab at Mcity, a semester-long internship for Michigan students with startups in the mobility industry. As part of this program, I worked with Tome to develop innovative solutions to prevent bicycle-vehicle collisions by leveraging connected infrastructure and machine learning.

Also in 2017, I worked with Professor Di Ma through the Multidisciplinary Design Program, researching intrusion detection for the Controller Area Network (CAN) bus in automobiles and developing a machine-learning based intrusion detection and prevention system for the CAN bus.
