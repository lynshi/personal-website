My name is Lyndon Shi and I am a Seth Bonder Undergraduate at the University of Michigan working under the direction of Professor Pascal Van Hentenryck. Currently I am researching optimal power flow problems with the help of the Grid Research for Good project.

At the University of Michigan, I am pursuing a B.S.E. in computer science and a minor in mathematics.

In Winter 2018, I was an Instructional Aide for ENGR 100-950, Electronics for Atmospheric and Space Measurements taught by Professor Aaron Ridley. Students in this class design and build a weather balloon payload housing a variety of atmospheric sensors. Payloads are deployed on a balloon to 100,000 ft, then recovered to analyze data collected by the integrated sensors. As an instructional aide for this class, I helped students design and solder printed circuit boards, program a microcontroller to interface with sensors and data logging devices, and build a thermally insulating and impact absorbing packaging. The multidisciplinary nature of the class curriculum is evident during this engineering process, and this semester-long position honed my abilities to lead and engineer in an environment beyond my core engineering discipline.

In Fall 2017, I was a member of TechLab at Mcity, a semester-long internship for Michigan students with startups in the mobility industry. As part of this program, I worked with Tome to develop innovative solutions to prevent bicycle-vehicle collisions by leveraging connected infrastructure and machine learning. In particular, I explored applying machine learning models towards identifying danger zones for bicyclists to help reduce the number of bicycle-vehicle collisions in the United States. I also helped develop proof-of-concept infrastructure for detecting bicyclists and warning nearby drivers.

In 2017, I worked with Professor Di Ma through the Multidisciplinary Design Program, researching intrusion detection for the Controller Area Network (CAN) bus in automobiles and developing a machine-learning based intrusion detection and prevention system for the CAN bus.
